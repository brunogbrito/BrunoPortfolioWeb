PickPon Game

November 4, 2018

Project Details

  • Game Engine:
    Unreal Engine 4
  • Production Time:
    2 weeks

  • Download Build

PickPon is a wild player versus player arena battle game for 2-4 players.


Face off against your friends in an intense and crazy showdown where being the fastest and smartest collector is the name of the game. Compete to pick up the most items before the timer runs out, but be careful! The other competitors can steal your items before you deposit them in your zone!
There are many different ways to play to get to the top, but only one can win the collectorbot championship!


*Controllers are required



Pickpon was our first school game project. I participated on this project as Game Designer and project manager. Our team was composed of 9 students from different disciplines (Design, 2D and 3D).

Our goal was to make a physical based game in two weeks.

Week 1


One of the biggest challenges in this project was to come up with a quick and simple game idea that could be produced in 2 weeks. Learning about our strengths and weaknesses was crucial to understand what we could do and what we should we avoid.


We took 2 days to rapid prototype some of the group ideas



 

Maybe too crazy...


Inspired on Katamari, we came up with a sphere collector racing game idea. Designers felt confortable in working with a character that could roll and attach objects on it instead dealing with complex bipeds or other physical bodies structures that could generate a lot of problems for a two week project.


At this stage I mostly worked with general game design and setting up the local multiplayer system for our game with possibility to have or not a split screen mode.


We were very excited with the idea and progress but not realizing how much over scoped it was. A lot of different mechanics (push, dash, jump, and even others that I can't remember anymore), pickups, different ways to interact between players.


 

Our milestone review was a disaster...

At the end of the first week we presented our game idea and prototype, but we got a bad review from the headteachers mostly regarding the gameplay and design. They didn’t like the idea of a racing game, “It will be too difficult to balance and probably frustrating for the players who get behind” they said. We came out of that meeting shocked and panicked.


In other hand it was good! After that meeting we downscoped the entire game simplifying it in a way that we managed to finish it in 4 days with good quality and playability!



Week 2


After long talks and brainstorming, we decided to simplify the game as much as we could. The game now became what we called Katamari Battleroyale. Short arena, where players would have to pick up many object as possible and drop them in their base area.


Most of the mechanics were implemented by them. This week I focused in script the main menu logic and general game loop rules.


 

Also worked in creating scripting tools for other designers to quickly create a crowd arena and helped 3D artists integrate directional light system with their shader inside the engine.


 

Reflection:

My biggest insecurity was the fear of failing my team for some reason, which fortunately did not happen, but anxiety was a very difficult factor to control during those days. I really enjoy scripting and creating tools to make it easier for other designers and I believe my scripts saved a lot of time for certain tasks that would take a lot of time if they were done manually. Overall it was a great experience, the team was quite pleased with the result!


It was a pleasure working with this team :)


 

John Walden, me and Josefine Folkenson - FG 18 | Game Design
